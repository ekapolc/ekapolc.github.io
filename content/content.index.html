Research Interest

 I have been working in Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) since undergrad where I was a part of the CMU Robust Speech Recognition Group. Then, I was in MIT's Spoken Language Systems group during my PhD years. I have worked in many parts of the ASR systems, such as robust features, voice activity detection, acoustic modeling, and language modeling. I also participated in the IARPA's Babel program as part of the BABELON team where I worked in keyword spotting and multilingual speech recognition using deep learning.

 Currently, I am part of the Spoken Language Systems group at Chulalongkorn University where I continue my work in keyword spotting. I am also part of the Assistive Technology Group where I apply machine learning techniques and ASR to provide technologies for disabled/eldery people.

 Lastly, I am also part of the Computational Molecular Biology Research Group working on Proteomics data analysis.
